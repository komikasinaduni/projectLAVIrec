About / Concept

Root vegetables are difficult to grow in standard aeroponic systems because existing supports restrict growth or require costly substrate. This limits yields for small farmers in resource-constrained areas.
PROJECT L.A.V.I. uses a modular mesh-based support system allowing roots to expand freely while receiving precise nutrient mist, enabling higher yields and low-cost deployment.

Impact / Vision

✰ Who benefits: Small-scale farmers in underdeveloped regions, their families, and local communities.

✰ Sustainable cycle: 5% of crop revenue goes back into maintaining and expanding the system, funding community infrastructure.

✰ Long-term goals and expansion plans: Scale units to 1,000+ farmers, optimize for multiple root crops, create open-source guides for replication.
